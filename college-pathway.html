Common Questions

FAQs

Can Australian players get basketball scholarships in the US?
Yes. Australian players are highly regarded in US college basketball — the international game is well respected, and Australian athletes tend to be disciplined, coachable, and physically developed. Both NCAA and NAIA programs actively recruit international players, and Australian athletes have been placing at US colleges for decades across all divisions.
What level do I need to be to get recruited?
It depends on which level you're targeting. D1 programs want elite, NBL1-level athletes or above. D2 and NAIA programs recruit strong state-level players. Junior colleges (JUCO) are a great entry point for players who need development time before moving to a four-year program. We assess your current level honestly and match you to realistic targets.
How old do I need to be to start the process?
The earlier the better — ideally athletes start the development and profile-building process at 15–16, with active recruitment outreach beginning at 16–17. NCAA rules restrict when coaches can contact athletes directly, but building your physical profile and highlight footage early gives you a significant advantage.
What academic requirements are there?
NCAA Division I and II programs have specific eligibility requirements including minimum GPA, core course requirements, and standardised test scores for international students. NAIA requirements are generally more flexible. We guide you through what's required for the programs you're targeting well in advance so academics don't become a barrier.
What is the difference between NCAA, NAIA, and JUCO?
NCAA (National Collegiate Athletic Association) is the largest governing body — D1 is the highest level, D2 offers strong scholarships with a lower profile. NAIA (National Association of Intercollegiate Athletics) governs smaller colleges and often has more flexibility for international students. JUCO (Junior College) is a two-year pathway — athletes compete and then transfer to a four-year program. Each has different scholarship structures, academic requirements, and competition levels.
Do I need to be in Melbourne to use this service?
No. The college pathway service is available to Australian athletes nationally. VALD testing is conducted at our Keilor Park facility for Melbourne-based athletes, and we can coordinate testing for interstate athletes. Profile building and coach outreach can be done remotely.
How long does the process take?
It varies. Some athletes receive offers within a few months of active outreach. Others take 12–18 months of development before they're ready to be marketed to programs. We'll give you an honest timeline based on your current level and goals from the first conversation.
